Creating a Transparent Bike Routes Image from "Northampton bike routes.png"
================================================================================

GOAL: Extract red bike routes from a satellite image and create a TIFF with
transparent background, preserving anti-aliasing and making routes suitable
as an overlay on other maps.

INITIAL STEPS:
1. Created TIFF copy of the PNG file:
   magick "Northampton bike routes.png" "Northampton bike routes.tif"

APPROACH:
The challenge was to isolate red bike routes while:
- Preserving smooth anti-aliased edges
- Removing grey-green streets and reddish-brown fields from background
- Making routes opaque and saturated enough to stand out as overlay
- Avoiding pixelation

FINAL WORKING COMMAND:
magick "Northampton bike routes.tif" \
  \( +clone -colorspace RGB \
     \( -clone 0 -separate -delete 1,2 \) \
     \( -clone 0 -separate -delete 0,2 \) \
     \( -clone 0 -separate -delete 0,1 \) \
     -delete 0 \
     \( -clone 1 -clone 2 -evaluate-sequence max \) \
     -delete 1,2 \
     -compose minus -composite \
     -level 35%,65% \
     -gamma 1.5 \
  \) \
  -alpha off -compose copy-opacity -composite \
  "Northampton bike routes transparent.tif"

HOW IT WORKS:
1. Separates RGB channels
2. Computes: Red - max(Green, Blue) to identify where red dominates
3. Applies level adjustment (35%,65%):
   - 35% cutoff removes grey-green streets and reddish-brown fields
   - 65% upper limit boosts opacity/saturation of routes
4. Applies gamma 1.5 to preserve smooth anti-aliased edges
5. Uses result as alpha channel (transparency mask)

KEY PARAMETERS:
- level 35%: Lower cutoff removes background with weak red dominance
- level 65%: Upper limit increases route opacity/saturation
- gamma 1.5: Preserves anti-aliasing (values >1 brighten mid-tones smoothly)

ITERATION NOTES:
- Hard thresholds removed anti-aliasing (made routes pixelated)
- Auto-level brought in too much background
- Gamma <1 (e.g., 0.5) made everything too transparent
- Multiplying RGB channels darkened routes (added black) rather than saturating
- Dilation widened routes but created grey pixels and brought back roads
- Final balance: 35%,65% level with gamma 1.5

RESULT:
"Northampton bike routes transparent.tif" - Red bike routes on transparent
background, smooth anti-aliased edges, suitable for overlay on other maps.
